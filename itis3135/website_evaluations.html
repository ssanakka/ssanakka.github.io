Website Evaluations

Good Website: Apple.com

Apple's website exemplifies excellent design principles with clean layouts, intuitive navigation, and premium aesthetics that reflect their brand identity.

  • Contrast: Excellent use of contrast with white space against product images, clear typography hierarchy, and strategic color accents that guide the eye.
  • Repetition: Consistent design patterns throughout the site, uniform navigation elements, repeated color schemes, and standardized product presentation layouts.
  • Alignment: Perfect grid-based alignment, symmetrical layouts, clean margins, and precise positioning of all elements creating visual harmony.
  • Proximity: Related elements grouped logically, appropriate spacing between sections, and clear visual relationships between content blocks.

Bad Website: LingsCars.com

Lings Cars is notoriously known for its chaotic design that violates virtually every web design principle with overwhelming visual noise and confusing navigation.

  • Contrast: Poor contrast with clashing colors, animated backgrounds competing with text, and no clear visual hierarchy making content unreadable.
  • Repetition: Inconsistent styling throughout, random font changes, unpredictable layout patterns, and no cohesive design system.
  • Alignment: Elements scattered randomly across the page, no grid structure, inconsistent margins, and chaotic positioning disrupting visual flow.
  • Proximity: Unrelated elements placed together, inadequate spacing, crowded layouts, and no logical grouping of content.
